SUBSYSTEM

BREAKING

Brakes, they are real lifesavers! When you need to stop or slow down, just step on that brake pedal, and the magic happens. The braking system uses hydraulic power to squeeze those brake pads against the wheels, creating friction, and bringing the vehicle to a smooth stop.When the brake pedal is applied the two curved brake shoes, which have a friction material lining, are forced by hydraulic wheel cylinders against the inner surface of a rotating brake drum. The result of this contact produces friction which enables the vehicle to slow down or stop.

SUSPENSION AND STEERING

The suspension system is like magic for a smooth ride! It's got springs and shock absorbers that soak up all those pesky bumps and vibrations, making your drive way more comfortable. And guess what? The steering system is the reason you can easily navigate the roads! With a turn of the steering wheel, you can gracefully glide around corners and steer.he primary function of your cars suspension and steering systems is to allow the wheels to move independently of the car, while keeping it “suspended” and stable. Any play or uncontrolled motion in these systems results in a deterioration of handling and accelerated tire wear.

TRANSMISSION

The mechanism that transmits the power developed by the engine of automobile to the engine to the driving wheels is called the TRANSMISSION SYSTEM (or POWER TRAIN).It's the secret behind your vehicle's power and gears! It cleverly shifts gears, allowing the engine to work efficiently and give you the right amount of power for your driving needs.In a car, a transmission, or a gearbox, acts as the medium that transmits power generated by the engine to the wheels via a mechanical system of gears and gear trains. A transmission allows a person to apply power to a vehicle in a controlled manner, without which, the car would not efficiently move.

ELECTRICAL AND ELECTRONICS

Electrical system is like the brain of your ride! It handles all the electrical stuff, from the engine to lights and entertainment. Battery, alternator, fuses, and wiring – they're all part of this superhero system. Without it, your vehicle would just be a bunch of metal parts with no life. A Car's electrical system is very complex in today's auto industries, everything from lights, brakes, accessories, ignitions, and throttles are all controlled electronically.
